Our Mission

University Unite hopes to broaden students’ academic horizons by connecting them to educational institutions across the world. At present, students are limited to learning what is presented to them by their own college or university. Certainly the information available to an individual through independent study is abundant, but it is the skillful presentation of knowledge by practiced professors that most effectively deepens one’s understanding of a subject. As a student, would it not be markedly beneficial to have access to information the way it is presented at other universities? University Unite aims to fill this informational void in two main ways, meeting the requirements laid out in the University Unite’s Method. The primary goal is to link students in a specific program of study to that program of study across the globe. Essentially, we want to facilitate the study abroad experience already encouraged by so many colleges and make it more personal, comfortable, and beneficial for the students participating. Additionally, University Unite wants to serve as a platform that opens up a dialogue between students from different schools in the same field of study as a way forming a sort of communal body of knowledge interlaced with peer support.

How would this look in practice? A student enters Stevens Institute majoring in Engineering Management. He takes a class in Informatics and looks online for some help during one of his assignments. He stumbles upon a Stevens page that informs him of a study abroad opportunity in the Netherlands available in the field and decides that he might be interested, He logs on to University Unite, enters his major, his desired location of study, and semesters during which he would be willing to study abroad. University Unite matches the students to all of the relevant programs being offered at international schools, featuring those universities offering study abroad programs during his preferred semesters. The student learns that not only are there opportunities in the Netherlands, but also at the University of Beijing Technology in China, where half of his family lives. The student takes the opportunity to explore his interest by chatting with students at the university, getting their feedback on the ways classes are taught, the environment, and the material covered. Hearing many positives, he finds a contact at the University of Beijing Technology through University Unite, and his study plans for the following semester are solidified!

Upcoming Steps

Find Your Educational Retreat

We encourage you to utilize the exploration tool on our site to find an academic home for yourself somewhere across the world. Our interactive map will help you to place yourself in a space where you feel comfortable, yet excited about being in a new space. In addition, our informational pages will give you more insight into the academics and campus lives of the different universities with whom we work. Be sure to read up before making a decision, and feel free to contact us if you come across any questions throughout your research. We would be happy to direct you to more detailed resources in order to round out and complete your search.

Experience the World

Make the most out of your study abroad experience. Classes are your primary focus, but studying abraod offers the opportunity to learn so much outside the classroom. We suggest that you immerse yourself completely in the culture. Taking an introductory language course based on the country to which you travel may be in your best interest, and indeed some universities require this. Your experience can be further rounded out by embracing the local cuisine, partaking in any local/national holidays that might be occurring during your studies. By doing so, you give yourself the opportunity to go home with a deeper understanding of the complexity of the human race.

Share Your Experience

University Unite wants to be the resource you need to make your study abroad decisions. But on the other end of the spectrum, we also want to be the place where you have the opportunity to share your experiences with others- the good, the bad, and the in between. The information we provide should serve only as a basis. The information YOU provide, however, will provide a more accurate representation of the individual programs which we describe. A simple description of your day-to-day activities could mean the world to another student currently in the process of planning their study abraod trip. Your posts are always welcome on our site, and we cannot wait to hear from you!

Study Abroad Q & A

Question One: What majors commonly study abroad?
The current top 5 fields for study abroad are:
  • STEM Fields
  • Business
  • Social Sciences
  • Foreign Language and International Studies
  • Fine Arts and Applied Artists
Question Two: What areas commonly host United States study abroad students?
The breakdown of countries hosting United States study abroad students is:
  • 54% Europe
  • 16% latin America and Caribbean
  • 11% Asia
  • 4% Sub-Saharan Africa
  • 4% Oceania
  • 2% Middle East and North Africa
  • 1% North America
Question Three: How long does a study abroad program last?
  • 63% of programs are short-term (eight or fewer weeks)
  • 35% of programs are mid-length (approximately one semester long)
  • 2% of programs are long term (up to one academic or calendar year)
